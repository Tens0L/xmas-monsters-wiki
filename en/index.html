01 / Introduction

Introduction

How the story begins, and how to read this compendium.

The story begins on Christmas Eve night. Our hero writes a letter to Santa — "I don't need any toys. I want to become a hero and watch an adventure movie of myself traveling the world" — then falls asleep, and wakes up collapsed in an unfamiliar forest. Right there, a tiny Slime is fighting off a bad monster. That's where the adventure — and the monster-collecting — begins.

The game is a build-free, zero-external-asset browser RPG: every 16×16 tile and sprite is generated entirely in code. The monster icons pasted throughout this compendium are drawn on the fly using that exact same generation logic.

You can switch the display language between English and にほんご any time from the title screen or the Settings menu (English is the default). This compendium lists every monster and skill name in both languages, so you won't get lost no matter which language you're playing in.

02 / Controls

Controls

That's the whole control scheme — the same on the field and in battle.

↑↓←→ / WASDMove / move cursor
SpaceConfirm / open mini-menu
X / ESCCancel

On the field, if there's something interactive in front of you — a person, a sign, a shop counter — Space skips the mini-menu and talks to it directly. Press Space facing empty ground and the mini-menu opens instead: Talk/Check · Monsters · Items · Settings · Close.

Formation, at the bottom of the Monsters menu, lets you freely arrange your party's starting positions within the left 3 columns of the same 10×10 grid used in battle. Decide your own front and back lines — put your fragile healer in the back row for safety.

03 / Walkthrough

Walkthrough

The single main path, from the opening to postgame content.

Meet the Slime in the forest

Help the Slime fighting a bad monster in the forest where you wake up, and a tutorial battle begins. Speech bubbles walk you through the battle screen step by step, so following along wins the fight. The Slime joins as your first party member afterward.

Arrive in Castella Town

Head north out of the forest and you'll reach the town. The inn, item shop, wizard's house, and ranch are all here — this becomes your home base from here on (details in the next chapter).

Talk to the King in the castle

Meet the King, and he asks you to clear out the monsters overrunning the cave behind the castle. Once the conversation ends, the guard steps aside and the cave becomes accessible.

Push through Cave B1 and B2

Two floors of random encounters. Near the entrance you'll only face a single weak enemy, but the deeper you go, the more enemies appear at once (up to 3) and the higher their level climbs. Grind a bit near the entrance before pushing further in.

Black Santa at the depths

The ruler of the cave, Black Santa, and his minions bar your way — a being who steals the dreams of good children. Bring a party that can exploit his elemental weakness.

The King's reward

After the fight you're warped before the King, who thanks you and gives you the legendary Holy Knight (Lv12) as a new party member.

Postgame POSTGAME

A reformed Black Santa now waits at the depths — beat him in a rematch and he'll finally join your party for real. Postgame also unlocks two true final fusion monsters, Chaos Dragon and Eve (see chapter 09).

04 / Town

Town

What you can do in Castella Town. Always swing back here between outings.

🛏 Inn

Record your journey to save, or Take a rest (20G) to fully restore your whole party's HP, MP, and status ailments. The menu order, top to bottom, is Take a rest → Record your journey → Never mind.

🎒 Item Shop

Buy and sell items. On the Buy screen, whichever item your cursor is on shows its description below.

  • Herb (HP +30)30G
  • Antidote (cures poison)10G
  • Magic Water (MP +20)80G
  • Return Wing (warp to town)25G
  • Power Seed (ATK +3)300G

🧪 Wizard's House

Put two monsters level 10 or higher into the magic pot and a new monster is born (details in chapter 09). You can also check the fusion chart here.

🐑 Ranch

A place to leave monsters when your party of 3 is full. Talk to the rancher to see a list of monsters currently staying there on the right side of the screen, and swap freely (you can't leave your very last monster).

05 / Battle System

Battle System

Automated tactics battles on a 10×10 grid. All you set is the "tactic."

When you're ambushed, your party lines up on the left and enemies on the right of a 10×10 grid. Turn order goes by highest Agility (AGI) first. Instead of commanding each monster's movement, attacks, and skills yourself, you assign one of the three "tactics" below to each monster and let them fight on their own.

Go all outガンガンいこう

Picks whatever deals the most damage (highest-priority skill), and 40% of the time targets the enemy with the lowest HP. Good for wanting a quick kill.

Mix it upいろいろやろう

Prioritizes healing if any ally is below 40% HP. A balanced style that mixes in poison skills a bit over 30% of the time and attack skills 50% of the time.

Stay safeいのちをだいじに

Heals any ally under 60% HP first, and falls back once its own HP drops below 25%. A cautious style, well suited to a dedicated healer.

Press Space mid-battle to change everyone's tactics starting next round (a control hint stays on screen at all times, too). Attacks spawn element-specific particle effects, and battle pacing can be tuned from Settings' Battle Speed (Slow / Normal / Fast) — the default is the somewhat unhurried "Normal."

There are a few status effects to know. Poison shaves off 8% of max HP every round; Bind (from skills like Root Bind) has a chance to lock a target out of its turn for one round; Iron Wall multiplies the caster's own Defense by 1.5 for 3 rounds.

06 / Elements

Elements

Seven elements — Fire, Water, Wood, Metal, Earth, Light, Dark. Damage shifts with the attacker/defender match-up.

Following the classic five-element cycle, Fire beats Metal, Metal beats Wood, Wood beats Earth, Earth beats Water, and Water beats Fire. On top of that, Light and Dark are each strong against the other. Attacking with a favorable element deals 1.5× damage; attacking into an unfavorable one deals 0.65×.

10 / Strategy

Strategy Tips

Things worth knowing that we picked up along the way.

01
Raising two of the same species to Lv10 guarantees an evolution into the advanced form. Fusion isn't a gamble — it's a breeding plan for deciding which line to invest in. Pick a target element first, then farm the wilds for matching monsters.
02
Skill MP costs are low across the board (basic skills run 2-5 MP), so feel free to cast freely from the very start. Carrying 1-2 Magic Water as a safety net for when you run dry is a good habit.
03
Put your "Mix it up" healer in the back (right-leaning columns) via Formation so it's less likely to eat the enemy's opening attack. High-DEF Golem- and Titan-line monsters make good front-liners.
04
Lingering near the cave entrance keeps enemies weak. Do your early gold-farming and skill leveling near the entrance, and only push deeper once your gear and party are ready.
05
Black Santa is a Dark-element boss. Light-element skills (Shine Flash / Holy Ray) land 1.5x damage on him. Keep a Holy Knight or a Star Kid-line monster in your lineup.
06
After the credits, always head back to the cave depths for a rematch with Black Santa. Beat him and he joins your party — which also unlocks the final fusion route, "Santa × Black Santa → Eve."
